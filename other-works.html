01 — equip.co.at

Repositioning an IT staffing firm as a human-first employer brand — without losing the corporate credibility that enterprise clients expect.

Equip GmbH is an Austrian IT personnel consulting firm founded in 2002, placing software developers, project managers, and IT specialists with enterprise clients across the DACH region. Their existing website had accumulated over fifteen years of incremental updates — inconsistent typography, an outdated grid, and marketing copy that spoke in staffing-industry clichés rather than to either candidate or client audiences.

The redesign project centred on two simultaneous audiences with opposing needs: enterprise clients who want reliability signals (certifications, process rigour, tenure), and candidates who want to sense culture and human connection. The design solution needed to hold both without compromising either.

Design Iterations

Four rounds from audit
to approved direction

V1 — Structural audit
Navigation & IA reset
Preserved the existing navy palette while restructuring the information architecture around two audience paths. Removed 40% of navigation items through card sorting with internal team.
V2 — Warm orange direction
Human warmth, orange accent
Introduced orange as a warmer accent to humanise the navy. Team responded positively to the warmth — but client stakeholders flagged it as "too startup" for their enterprise positioning. Direction abandoned.
V3 — Teal & deep navy
Employer brand alignment
Teal replaced orange — credible enough for enterprise, human enough for candidates. Four-value icon row introduced to anchor the "Menschlichkeit, Vertrauen, Offenheit, Stabilität" messaging. Direction approved for refinement.
V4 — Final direction
Refined and approved
Photography integration, teal CTA against deep navy, certification badges added to footer. Eyebrow label pattern added above hero headline. Four-value cards with icon + heading + two-line description. Delivered to development.
