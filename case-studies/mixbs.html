01 — Brief

MiXBS is a US-based contact centre outsourcing company with 15 years of experience, recognised by DesignRush as one of the top 10 contact centres in Florida. The company builds and manages expert remote teams for customer service, marketing support, and business process outsourcing — training, managing, and delivering measurable results across industries including logistics, healthcare, retail, and events.

At the core of MiXBS operations is BayCX — the company's proprietary remote working software. BayCX is designed to increase customer engagement and boost sales by giving agents and operations teams a unified platform for managing interactions, workflows, and performance. The platform handles up to 14,000–16,000 daily interactions for clients such as CDEK, covering inbound and outbound calls across multiple channels and languages.

My engagement as UX Consultant ran from 2022 to 2025, covering platform architecture mapping, visual design exploration, design system definition, component development, and mobile authentication flows.

02 — System Architecture

Before designing any screen, the platform's service architecture needed to be mapped. BayCX is built around a central Orchestrator layer that coordinates all platform modules: scheduling (Scheduler, Calendar), operational processing (Factory, PBX Gateway, Inquiries, Tickets), data services (PKI, Contacts, ChatDesk, GLAP), and infrastructure (SMS, Email, Notifications, Auth Server, Analytics).

Mapping these dependencies was the foundation for every interaction design decision — particularly for understanding which modules drove the authentication flow, how session state moved through the system, and where communication channel handoffs occurred. The user flow diagram covers the login and password recovery sequence: entry, forgotten credentials, verification via OTP, and setting a new password.

03 — Visual Exploration

Before finalising the visual direction, multiple colour themes were explored systematically across identical interface layouts. Five distinct palettes — spanning the approved teal direction, purple, deep navy, maroon, and green — were applied to the same BayCX screens and compared side by side.

This approach made it straightforward to evaluate readability, brand alignment, and usability without the distraction of layout variation. The matrix format — same screens, different colour systems — was presented to stakeholders and became the basis for selecting and refining the final Poppy Glow teal direction.

04 — Design System

The BayCX design system defines two primary colour families: Poppy Glow — a teal range across 5 steps from #E6F1F2 to #022F32 — and Kanz Orange — 5 steps from #FFF0E6 to #662900. A 9-step neutral grey scale (#5F738C to #F5F5F5) handles all UI surface and text states, alongside a two-tone black/white pair (#FFFFFF / #192427) for base contrast.

The button component covers all states across both brand colours: 5 sizes (extra small through extra large), 6 variants (filled, filled with icon, text-only, text with icon, outlined, outlined with icon), and full dark-background support — giving developers a complete, consistent token set across every context the platform uses.

05 — Authentication

The mobile authentication flow covers the full password recovery sequence across six screens: selecting the recovery method (email or phone), receiving and entering a time-limited OTP code, handling code expiry with a resend option, verifying the code, and setting a new password with inline validation feedback.

The flow prioritises clarity at each step — progressive disclosure keeps each screen to a single task, error states are specific rather than generic, and the keyboard layout is matched to the input type (numeric for OTP, standard for email input) to reduce friction on mobile.
