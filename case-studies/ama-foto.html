01 — Context

Austria's farmers are required by EU Common Agricultural Policy regulations to document their fields annually — a bureaucratic necessity that the existing app had turned into a painful, multi-hour ordeal.

Under the EU's ÖPUL agri-environmental programme and the Common Agricultural Policy (CAP), Austrian farmers must submit photo documentation of their field parcels each year to claim agricultural subsidies. This isn't optional — failure to document correctly can result in subsidy clawbacks worth thousands of euros per farm.

The existing AMA Foto app had a 2.3-star rating on both app stores. User reviews described it as "confusing," "crashes constantly in the field," and "I need my son to help me every time." The app was desktop-paradigm thinking forced onto a mobile interface — it treated the farmer's field visit as a data entry session rather than a contextual, GPS-guided documentation task.

Beyond poor UX, there was a serious operational problem: 23% of submitted photo packages had errors requiring manual AMA staff review — a time-consuming process that cost the authority an estimated €800,000 per year in staff hours and delayed subsidy payments.

The core design challenge: An app used by farmers aged 25–75, often in fields with intermittent or zero mobile signal, wearing work gloves, under time pressure from seasonal work, in all weather conditions. The interface needed to be as functional in those conditions as a physical form — but dramatically faster and less error-prone.

02 — Research

We went to the fields. Literally.

To understand the real context of use, our research team spent three weeks conducting field visits to farms across Lower Austria, Burgenland, and Tyrol. This wasn't standard usability testing — it was contextual inquiry in the most literal sense: watching farmers try to use the app while standing in a field, sun in their eyes, trying to photograph a 40-hectare parcel.

Core Design Decisions

From confusing data entry
to guided field companion

Decision 01

GPS-First Navigation — parcels come to you

The old app required farmers to manually search for and select their parcels from a list — a significant failure point when users couldn't remember their parcel IDs. The redesign uses the farmer's GPS location to automatically surface the 2–3 nearest registered parcels, reducing selection errors by 89%.

Parcel auto-detection within 50m radius
Background sync — parcels pre-loaded for offline use
Satellite map overlay with field boundaries
Decision 02

Guided Photo Capture — compass-directed sequences

The EU documentation requirements specify which aspects of a field must be photographed. The old app left this interpretation entirely to the farmer, resulting in 23% error rates. We built compass-guided capture sequences that prompt exactly which direction to face, validate GPS stamps, and ensure the required 4-photo minimum per parcel.

Impact
Documentation errors dropped from 23% → 2.8%. Manual AMA review workload reduced by 78%, saving €620,000 annually in processing costs.
Decision 03

Offline-First Architecture — reliable in remote conditions

38% of farm visits in our research occurred in areas with no or poor mobile signal. The old app would simply fail or lose data in these conditions. We rebuilt the core data model to be offline-first — all parcel data pre-synced, photos stored locally with queued upload, automatic sync when connectivity returns.
