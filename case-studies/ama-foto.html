01 — Context

Austria's farmers are required by EU Common Agricultural Policy regulations to document their fields annually — and in 2021 there was no mobile way to do it in the field.

Under the EU's ÖPUL agri-environmental programme and the Common Agricultural Policy (CAP), Austrian farmers must submit photo documentation of their field parcels each year to claim agricultural subsidies. This isn't optional — failure to document correctly can result in subsidy clawbacks worth thousands of euros per farm.

Until 2021, all documentation had to go through the AMA MFA web portal — a desktop-first platform designed for subsidy applications. Farmers would visit their fields, take photos on their phones, then manually upload them later at home or at the office. The process was disconnected, prone to errors, and wholly unsuited to fieldwork conditions.

AMA Foto was created as a dedicated mobile companion to the MFA portal — the first purpose-built tool for in-field documentation. There was no previous version to iterate on; we were designing for a workflow that had never had a proper digital solution. 23% of submitted photo packages had errors requiring manual AMA staff review, costing the authority an estimated €800,000 per year in staff hours and delaying subsidy payments.

The core design challenge: An app used by farmers aged 25–75, often in fields with intermittent or zero mobile signal, wearing work gloves, under time pressure from seasonal work, in all weather conditions. The interface needed to be as functional in those conditions as a physical form — but dramatically faster and less error-prone.

02 — Research

We went to the fields. Literally.

To understand the real context of use, our research team spent three weeks conducting field visits to farms across Lower Austria, Burgenland, and Tyrol. This wasn't standard usability testing — it was contextual inquiry in the most literal sense: watching farmers try to use the app while standing in a field, sun in their eyes, trying to photograph a 40-hectare parcel.

Decision 01

Contextual Field Detail — map, obligations, and data in one view

Opening a parcel immediately shows its satellite GIS boundary, registered crop data, and any open obligations as actionable cards — like a missing Blühzeitpunkt date surfaced at the bottom of the screen. Farmers see exactly what still needs doing on this specific field without switching between separate lists or sections.

Satellite map with exact parcel polygon overlay
Pending obligations surfaced as contextual action cards
Four focused tabs: Auftrag, Details, Unterlagen, Korrektur
Decision 02

Structured Correction Flow — review changes before they're submitted

When field attributes need updating — crop type, Schlagcode, Begrünungsvariante — the Korrektur tab presents a precise from/to comparison. The farmer sees exactly what will change, with explicit confirm and discard actions before anything is sent to AMA. Corrections can be annotated with a comment, creating an auditable trail for every change.

Impact
The from/to structure replaced ambiguous free-form edits. Documentation errors dropped from 23% → 2.8%, reducing manual AMA review workload by 78% and saving €620,000 annually.
Decision 03

Priority-Driven Task Queue — deadlines visible before you set off

During peak inspection season, an inspector may manage hundreds of open assignments. The Auftragsliste groups urgent items separately, shows deadline countdowns alongside distance, and tags each card with the obligation type — SAT or Blühzeitpunkt. Sorting by Datum, Entfernung, or Typ lets users plan an efficient route without opening individual records.
